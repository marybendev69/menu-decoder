Antipasti

Starters
Sharing plates of local charcuterie, cheeses, and seasonal vegetables from the hills around Ancona and the Macerata interior.
Degustazione di Salumi e Formaggi
€13
Tasting board of cured meats & cheeses
A sharing platter of locally produced salumi and cheeses from the Marchigiano hills. Expect a selection of prosciutto, salame, lonza, and possibly ciauscolo (a spreadable pork salame unique to Le Marche), alongside aged pecorino, fresh caciotta, and other regional cheeses. Perfect for two to share while you decide on the rest.
Great for Sharing
Degustazione di Formaggi
€12
Cheese tasting board
A cheese-only selection showcasing the dairy traditions of the Marche and central Italy — likely including fresh and aged pecorino, caciotta, and possibly a creamy stracchino or gorgonzola. Served with honey or mostarda.
Prosciutto Crudo Nostrano
€12
Local dry-cured ham
"Nostrano" means "our own" — this is locally produced prosciutto crudo, likely from small producers in the hills south of Ancona. Compared to Parma or San Daniele, Marchigiano prosciutto tends to be slightly more savoury and rustic with a firmer texture.
Bresaola Rucola e Grana
€12
Air-dried beef with arugula & aged Grana cheese
Thinly sliced air-dried, salt-cured beef from the Valtellina tradition, draped over peppery arugula and topped with shavings of aged Grana Padano. Typically dressed with olive oil and a squeeze of lemon. Lean, delicate, and elegant — a classic Italian light starter.
Manzarda di Manzo al Sale con Burratina Pugliese
€20
Salt-cured beef with Puglian burrata
A rare delicacy — manzarda is a traditional Marchigiano salt-cured beef preparation, similar to bresaola but with a more intensely savoury, concentrated flavour. Paired with a small burrata from Puglia, which adds a luscious, creamy contrast. The salt of the beef against the sweet cream of the burrata is a beautiful combination. One of the more luxurious starters on the menu.
Signature Dish
Burratina Pugliese
€6
Small Puglian burrata
A small burrata cheese from Puglia — a pouch of fresh mozzarella filled with stracciatella (shredded mozzarella in cream). When you cut it open, the creamy interior spills out. Simple, rich, and perfect with good bread and olive oil.
Insalata di Mela con Rucola, Noci, Pinoli e Aceto Balsamico
€6
Apple salad with arugula, walnuts, pine nuts & balsamic vinegar
A crisp, refreshing salad of sliced apple over peppery arugula, scattered with walnuts and pine nuts, and dressed with balsamic vinegar. Sweet, bitter, and nutty — a lighter start if you're saving room for the grill or a rich primo.
Parmigiana di Melanzane
€8
Eggplant parmesan
Layers of fried eggplant slices baked with tomato sauce, mozzarella, basil, and Parmigiano. A southern Italian classic that's beloved across the whole country. Expect a rich, bubbling, golden-topped portion — hearty enough to be a meal on its own.
Frittatina al Tartufo Nero Estivo
€15
Small frittata with summer black truffle
A delicate egg frittata infused with summer black truffle (tartufo nero estivo). The eggs provide a gentle, custardy base that lets the earthy truffle flavour come through beautifully. The Marche, especially near Acqualagna, is prime truffle territory, so this is about as local as it gets.

Le Cresce

Marchigiano Flatbreads
Crescia is a traditional Marche flatbread, historically made during harvest, grape-picking, and pig slaughter — when hearty appetites needed satisfying. Cooked on a hot griddle, it pairs beautifully with field herbs, local cured meats, and cheeses from these hills. You won't find this outside the region.

Primi Piatti

First Courses — Handmade Pasta
All pastas are handmade in-house in the Marchigiano tradition. Portions are generous. Several dishes offer a choice of pasta shape — ask your server what's freshest today.
Tagliatelle o Gnocchi al Battuto Maceratese
€10
Tagliatelle or gnocchi with Macerata-style ragù
Battuto maceratese is a slow-cooked meat ragù specific to the Macerata province. Unlike Bolognese, it's built on finely hand-chopped (not ground) pork, veal, and aromatics including celery, braised until rich and dense. The name "battuto" refers to chopping the meat by hand with a mezzaluna. A true local signature.
Local Signature
Gnocchi, Tagliolini o Tagliatelle alla Papera
€10
Gnocchi, tagliolini, or tagliatelle with duck ragù
"Papera" means duck in Marchigiano dialect. The meat is simmered until it falls apart into a rich, slightly gamey sauce — lighter than beef or pork ragù. Duck was commonly raised on the farms of central Italy alongside pigs and geese.
Pappardelle al Ragù di Cinghiale in Bianco
€12
Pappardelle with wild boar ragù, no tomato
Wide ribbony egg pappardelle with slow-braised wild boar "in bianco" — without tomato, so the deep, woodsy flavour of the cinghiale comes through. The boar is likely sourced from the nearby Sibillini Mountains, marinated in wine, juniper, and bay leaf before braising for hours. Rich, earthy, distinctly Marchigiano.
Recommended
Ravioli, Tagliatelle o Passatelli alla Norcina
€11
Ravioli, tagliatelle, or passatelli in Norcia-style sauce
"Alla norcina" is a sauce from Norcia in Umbria: crumbled pork sausage, black truffle, and cream. The passatelli option is especially interesting — short, worm-shaped strands made from breadcrumbs, Parmigiano, egg, and nutmeg, traditionally served in broth but here "asciutti" (dry) with the creamy sauce. A collision of three Italian regions.
Foglie Verdi Rucola, Grana e Pachino
€12
Green leaf pasta with arugula, Grana cheese & cherry tomatoes
"Foglie verdi" are flat pasta shapes cut to resemble leaves, with spinach or herbs worked into the dough. Tossed simply with peppery arugula, salty Grana, and sweet Sicilian cherry tomatoes. A lighter, fresher option compared to the rich ragùs.
Triangoli con Verdure a Vapore e Tartufo Nero
€13
Triangle stuffed pasta with steamed vegetables & black truffle
Triangular filled pasta parcels served with steamed seasonal vegetables and shaved summer black truffle. The Marche — especially Acqualagna — is Italy's truffle heartland. Summer black truffle is milder than winter white, with a gentle earthy aroma.
Cappellacci di Bufala con Pachino e Crema di Basilico Heather's Pick
€14
Buffalo mozzarella cappellacci with cherry tomatoes & basil cream
Cappellacci — large "ugly hat" shaped stuffed pasta — filled with creamy buffalo mozzarella, sauced with sweet Pachino cherry tomatoes and vibrant basil cream. Like a deconstructed caprese in pasta form. Expect a burst of warm, melty mozzarella inside each parcel. Rich but bright.
Ravioli o Tagliatelle al Tartufo Nero Estivo
€16
Ravioli or tagliatelle with summer black truffle
The truffle showcase — handmade pasta dressed generously with summer black truffle, likely in a butter and Parmigiano base. At €16 this is remarkably fair pricing; the Marche's proximity to Acqualagna means short supply chains and fresh truffle.
Truffle
Saraceni con Rucola, Grana e Pachino
€12
Buckwheat pasta with arugula, Grana cheese & cherry tomatoes
"Saraceni" means buckwheat pasta — grano saraceno ("Saracen grain") arrived in Italy via medieval trade routes. Despite the name, buckwheat isn't actually wheat; it's a seed related to rhubarb. It gives the pasta a nutty, earthy, toasty flavour and darker colour. More rustic and robust than the similar foglie verdi dish.
Orecchiette con Fava, Alici e Pomodorini
€11
Orecchiette with fava beans, anchovies & cherry tomatoes
A nod to Puglia — orecchiette ("little ears") are small concave pasta shapes, here combined with fresh fava beans, umami-rich anchovies that melt into the sauce, and sweet pomodorini. The anchovies dissolve during cooking, leaving behind savoury depth. A lighter, more Mediterranean option on a menu that otherwise leans meaty.
Strengozzi Guanciale e Pepe
€10
Hand-torn pasta with cured pork cheek & black pepper
Strengozzi are thick, rough-textured hand-torn noodles from the Umbria-Marche border. The name supposedly derives from "strangling." Think of this as the local riff on Rome's gricia: rendered guanciale (cured pork jowl) tossed with cracked black pepper, bound by starchy pasta water and cheese. The pasta's rough surface grabs every bit of sauce.
Sleeper Hit

Secondi Piatti al Forno

Oven-Baked Mains
Slow-cooked and oven-roasted dishes. The coniglio, oca, and maialino require advance ordering — ask your server if they're available tonight.
Scamorza al Forno con Radicchio
€10
Baked scamorza cheese with radicchio
Scamorza is a semi-soft, slightly smoked stretched-curd cheese (similar to mozzarella but firmer). Here it's baked until golden and bubbling, served over bitter radicchio. The sweetness of the melted cheese against the bitter green is a great contrast. Effectively vegetarian.
Coniglio in Porchetta
€15
Rabbit roasted "porchetta-style" — min. 4 people, pre-order
"In porchetta" means the rabbit is deboned, stuffed with garlic, fennel, rosemary, and herbs (the same treatment as the famous porchetta pig), then rolled and slow-roasted. Incredibly aromatic and tender. A true Marchigiano speciality — requires advance ordering for a minimum of 4 guests.
Pre-Order Only
Oca al Forno
€22
Roast goose — min. 4 people, pre-order
Whole roasted goose — a farmhouse tradition in the Marche countryside, where geese were raised alongside pigs. Rich, dark, flavourful meat with crispy skin. Requires advance ordering for a minimum of 4.
Pre-Order Only
Maialino allo Spiedo
€20
Spit-roasted suckling pig — min. 6 people, pre-order
A whole young pig slowly turned on a spit over embers. Crispy, crackling skin and incredibly tender, juicy meat. A celebration dish — requires advance ordering for at least 6 guests.
Pre-Order Only

Pan-Cooked Mains
Seared and sauced — refined preparations with more complex flavour profiles.
Medaglioni di Manzo con Pere e Agro

Beef medallions with pears & sweet-sour sauce
Thick-cut beef medallions (likely from the fillet or sirloin) pan-seared and served with poached pears in an "agro" — a traditional Italian sweet-and-sour reduction. The sweetness of the pear and the tangy sauce balance the rich beef beautifully. An elegant, refined dish.

Lamb braised in rum
An unusual and intriguing preparation — lamb pieces slow-braised with rum, which adds a deep, caramelised sweetness to the gamey meat. The alcohol cooks off, leaving behind complex, warming flavours. Not something you see often — worth trying if you're adventurous.

Secondi Piatti alla Brace

From the Charcoal Grill
The heart of the menu. Everything here is cooked over live charcoal — arrosticini, scottadito, fillets, and massive Marchigiana beef steaks. This is what the kitchen is proudest of.
Grigliata Mista
€16
Mixed grill — mutton skewer, lamb, sausage, ribs, capocollo & pork liver
The full tour of the grill: arrosticino di castrato (mutton skewer), lamb, salsiccia (pork sausage), costine (pork ribs), capocollo (pork neck), and fegatino (pork liver). A generous sampling platter that represents the whole central Italian grilling tradition. Outstanding value.
Great Value
Agnello Scottadito
€18
"Burn your fingers" lamb chops
Tiny lamb rib chops grilled fast over fierce heat and eaten with your hands while still scorching hot — that's what "scottadito" means. Just salt, olive oil, and fire. One of the most iconic dishes of central Italy. Charred outside, pink and juicy inside.
Must Try
Costine di Agnello

Lamb ribs
A larger, meatier cut than the scottadito — full lamb ribs grilled over charcoal. More meat to work with, same wonderful charred-lamb flavour.
Arrosticini di Castrato (10 pz)
€10
Mutton skewers — 10 pieces
Small cubes of castrato (mature mutton) threaded on thin wooden skewers and grilled over charcoal. Originally from Abruzzo, now beloved across the central Apennines. The flavour is deeper and more complex than lamb — slightly gamey, smoky, addictive. You'll want more than ten.
Spadino del Cavaliere (per 2 persone)
€30
The Knight's Sword — large meat skewer for 2
A dramatic, sword-length skewer loaded with alternating pieces of beef and pork sausage, grilled over charcoal. Designed and priced for two people to share. A fun, celebratory centerpiece if you want to go big.
For Two
